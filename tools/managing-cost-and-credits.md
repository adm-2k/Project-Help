# Managing Cost and Credits

_Last verified: 2026-06. Prices and free tiers change often, so re-check the linked sites and update this page each cohort (about quarterly)._

Money is the part people quietly worry about, so let's settle it up front. Most teams build their first project for free, or for a few dollars. Nobody is going to wake up to a surprise bill. This page explains how the billing actually works so that nothing about it catches you off guard, and so you can build without one eye on the meter.

## What you are paying for

AI builders do not usually charge a flat monthly fee for "all you can build." Most of them charge by how much work the AI does on your behalf, measured in either *[tokens](../GLOSSARY.md)* or *[credits](../GLOSSARY.md)*. Think of it like a taxi meter rather than a bus ticket: a bus ticket is one flat price no matter how far you go, but a meter ticks up with the trip. A short, clear request is a short trip. A long or complicated one, like asking the AI to rebuild a whole screen from scratch, costs a little more.

A *token* is a small chunk of text, roughly a few characters or part of a word. The AI reads your request and writes its answer in tokens, and tools that bill this way count them up. A *credit* is a simpler unit some tools use instead: one request or one action spends some credits, and you start each month or each day with a set amount. You do not need to do the math yourself. Both are just ways of saying the same thing: the more you ask the AI to chew on, the more it costs, and a small ask costs a small amount.

## What a first project really costs

Here is the honest version. For most teams, a first *[MVP](../GLOSSARY.md)* fits inside the free tier and costs nothing. The free amounts the popular tools give you are enough to describe an app, build it, fix it a few times, and get it live.

If your project is busy enough to outgrow the free tier, the common next step is a paid plan at around $25 a month. That is the headline figure for the popular browser builders. On top of a plan, a heavy day of building, where you are prompting the AI constantly for hours, might add a few dollars of usage. That is the whole picture. This is a small, controllable cost that you can see and steer, not a scary unknown that balloons while your back is turned.

## How to watch your usage

You are not flying blind. Almost every tool shows a usage meter or a billing dashboard, usually a click or two from the main screen, where you can see how much of your free tier or paid allowance you have used. Find it on day one so you know where it lives before you ever need it.

The habit that keeps everyone calm: before you start a building session, set a rough number in your head, something like "I am fine spending up to a few dollars today." Check the meter early, once you have done a little work, so you learn what a normal session costs. Then check it again after a big push. Do that a couple of times and you will always know roughly where you stand, which means no nasty surprises.

## Habits that keep costs low

Cost mostly follows from how you work, and a few simple habits keep it low without you thinking about it much.

- **Ask for small, clear tasks.** A tight request ("add a Save button to this form") is cheaper than a vague one ("make the app better"), because the AI does less guessing and you do fewer expensive re-dos.
- **Start a fresh session when one gets long.** When a conversation drags on, the AI has to re-read the whole muddled history each time, which costs more and works worse. Beginning a clean session for the next task is cheaper and clearer.
- **Don't ride a doom loop.** If the AI keeps fixing one thing and breaking another in circles, stop. That loop burns money fast and rarely escapes on its own. Step back and work the calm steps in [Getting unstuck](../guides/getting-unstuck.md) instead.
- **Use a cheaper model for simple work.** A *[model](../GLOSSARY.md)* is the particular AI brain doing the work, and many tools let you pick from a few. The bigger ones are smarter but cost more; the smaller, faster ones are plenty for routine jobs. Save the heavy model for genuinely hard problems and let a lighter one handle the simple stuff.

## If your spend spikes

If you ever glance at the meter and the number is higher than you expected, don't panic. Work these three steps, in order:

1. **Stop building.** Pause the current session. Nothing gets worse while you are not prompting.
2. **Check the usage meter.** Open the dashboard and look at what ran up the cost. It is usually one thing, often a long doom-loop session, and seeing it explains the number.
3. **Switch to the free tier or a cheaper model, and carry on.** Drop back to a lighter setup and keep going at a calmer pace. You rarely need the most expensive option to finish the job.

The reassuring part: you can always pause. The AI builder itself does not run up a bill while you are not using it, so closing the tab and coming back tomorrow costs nothing.

> Tip: A separately hosted app or database can have its own small running cost even while you sleep, but that is the hosting bill, not the AI builder. The free tiers in the [starter stack](README.md) are built to avoid that on a first project.
